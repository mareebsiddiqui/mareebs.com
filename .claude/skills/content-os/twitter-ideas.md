## Category 2: Quick Tips (30 ideas)

One actionable insight a founder can use today.

### Pain A - No CTO for AI Infrastructure (8)

31. Before hiring any technical leader for your fashion brand, ask one question: "Have you operated a system in production for 6+ months?" Past tense only.
32. Ask your AI vendor: "What happens when your system fails at 2am? Who gets paged?" If the answer is "you," ask what monitoring they provide.
33. Check your AI features right now. Is there a dashboard showing accuracy and uptime? If not, you're flying blind.
34. Before signing with any AI vendor, ask: "Show me your runbook for when recommendations go stale." No runbook means no production readiness.
35. Every Monday, ask your tech team: "What broke last week that we didn't notice?" The honest answer reveals your monitoring gaps.
36. Before evaluating any VTO vendor, check if your product data has standardised measurements and 3D-ready attributes. Without that, every demo is a lie.
37. When reviewing technical proposals, look for the section on "what happens when this fails." If it's missing, the proposal is incomplete.
38. Ask your developers: "If I woke you at 3am, could you diagnose and fix our recommendation engine in 30 minutes?" That's your production readiness test.

### Pain B - DPP Deadline, No Technical Leadership (8)

39. Ask your DPP vendor one question: "Show me a test submission to the registry." If they can't, they're selling you a mockup, not compliance.
40. Check if your product identifiers are GS1 GTINs today. If they're internal codes, your entire DPP data model will need rebuilding.
41. Open your product data system. Pick 10 random products. Check if material composition sums to 100%. That's your data quality baseline.
42. Ask your sustainability team: "Can we submit one product to the DPP registry today?" The answer reveals exactly how far you are.
43. Before building any DPP system, read the GS1 Digital Link standard. Your entire identifier scheme depends on it.
44. Count how many of your products have complete, verified fibre composition data. That percentage is your DPP readiness score.
45. Ask your DPP vendor: "What happens when the regulation updates and required fields change?" If they say "new project," run.
46. Pick your best-selling product. Try to assemble every data field the DPP registry requires for it. Time yourself. That gap is your compliance risk.

### Pain C - Supply Chain Data in Spreadsheets (7)

47. Start your supply chain audit with one question per supplier: "Can you send batch-level origin data in a structured digital format?" The silence tells you everything.
48. Open your top supplier's most recent data file. Count: how many fields are empty, how many formats differ from last month, how many require interpretation.
49. Ask each supplier: "If I need updated composition data for 100 products by Friday, can you deliver?" Their answer is your traceability timeline.
50. Find the person in your company who understands your supply chain spreadsheets. If there's only one, that's a single point of failure.
51. Pick 5 products. Try to trace materials back to origin using only your current data. Time it. If it takes more than 10 minutes per product, you have a problem.
52. Send the same data request to 3 suppliers. Compare what you get back. The format differences are your integration challenge.
53. Map every system that holds product data in your company. If the count exceeds 4 and none of them sync, you have a data architecture problem.

### Pain D - Can't Ship AI Features Fast Enough (7)

54. Pick your top 100 products. Count how many have 100% of attributes filled. If the number is below 80, fix data before starting AI.
55. Ask your data team: "What percentage of our product attributes are complete, consistent, and verified?" If they don't know, that's the answer.
56. Before buying any AI tool, export your product data. If more than 20% of fields are empty, the AI will perform worse than random.
57. Check when your AI model was last retrained. If nobody knows, the model is using stale data and recommendations are degrading daily.
58. Ask your AI vendor: "What minimum data completeness do you need for accurate results?" Then check if you meet that threshold.
59. Look at your last 30 days of AI-powered recommendations. Did anyone check accuracy? If the answer is no, you might be serving garbage.
60. Run one test: give your recommendation engine a product with missing attributes. If it still recommends confidently, it's guessing, not learning.
