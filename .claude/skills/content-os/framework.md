# Content OS Framework

Use this framework when generating new monthly content plans via `/content-os plan-next-month`.

## Cadence

- 1 blog post per day (7 days/week)
- 1 LinkedIn post per day (7 days/week)
- 1 Twitter/X post per day (7 days/week)
- = 180 blogs + 180 LinkedIn posts + 180 Twitter posts over 180 days
- Start date: May 8, 2026

## LinkedIn Pillar Rotation

Rotate pillars daily in a 5-day cycle:

| Day in cycle | Pillar |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | War Story |
| Day 2 | Myth-Busting |
| Day 3 | Framework & Playbook |
| Day 4 | Behind-the-Scenes |
| Day 5 | Founder Education |

Cycle repeats continuously regardless of day of week.

## Twitter/X Format

Twitter posts are shorter and punchier than LinkedIn. Same 5 pillars, same 4 pain points, but adapted to the platform:

- Max 280 characters for single tweets
- Thread format (3-5 tweets) allowed once per week for deeper topics
- More direct, less storytelling structure
- Can amplify/tease that day's blog or LinkedIn post
- Standalone observations and hot takes welcome

Twitter rotates through these tones:
1. Hot Take - Contrarian opinion, 1-2 sentences
2. Quick Tip - One actionable insight
3. Pattern - "I keep seeing X" observation
4. Question - Engagement hook
5. Reframe - Flip a common belief
6. One-Liner - Punchy standalone truth

## Blog Post Priority Queue

Generate blog topics in this priority order (spread across 180 days):

**Tier 1: Commercial Intent (days 1-30)**
People searching for the solution. High conversion potential.
- Fractional CTO for FashionTech comparisons (vs full-time, vs agency, vs doing nothing)
- "What does a fractional CTO for FashionTech do" explainers
- DPP compliance technical requirements guides
- Supply chain traceability architecture for fashion

**Tier 2: Symptom-First (days 15-90)**
People searching for the problem. They don't know the solution yet.
- "How do I prepare for DPP by 2027"
- "Supply chain data is in spreadsheets"
- "Agency doesn't understand DPP regulations"
- "Can't get supplier data into one system"
- "Competitors shipping AI features we can't match"

**Tier 3: Educational / Top-of-Funnel (days 30-150)**
People learning. Builds authority and SEO footprint.
- DPP regulation explained for non-technical founders
- GS1 standards decoded (GTIN, EPCIS, Digital Link)
- Supply chain data architecture concepts
- AI for fashion (predictive inventory, VTO, personalization)
- NFC/RFID data carriers explained simply
- ESPR regulation breakdown

**Tier 4: Case Studies (mix in 2-3 per week ongoing)**
Proof of results. Convert warm leads.
- Securiti.ai: compliance platform, 90% fewer errors
- ByteCorp: IoT device management (same as DPP data carriers)
- Wise: billions of data events, zero downtime
- Dubizzle: 60% revenue growth via AI personalization
- Before/after architecture transformation stories

## Topic Generation Rules

1. Each LinkedIn post must tie to one of the 4 pain points:
   - (A) No CTO for AI infrastructure (blind decisions on predictive inventory, VTO, personalization, agentic commerce)
   - (B) DPP deadline with no technical leadership (GS1 standards, data carriers, traceability, registry integration)
   - (C) Supply chain data in spreadsheets (fragmented across suppliers, no interoperability, can't build traceability)
   - (D) Can't ship AI features fast enough (competitors moving to agentic commerce, investors want operational rigor)

2. Blog posts alternate between pain points (never 3 in a row on the same one)

3. Every blog post should be repurposable: extract 2-3 insights that become LinkedIn and Twitter posts

4. Mix "teach" and "story" posts. Never 3 teaching posts in a row on LinkedIn (stories break it up)

5. Twitter posts can be extracted from blog/LinkedIn content OR standalone observations

6. At least 2 days per week, the LinkedIn post should directly relate to that day's blog topic (cross-promote)

7. Twitter can tease/amplify the blog or stand alone

## Pain Point Distribution (per week of 7 days)

- Pain A (No CTO for AI infrastructure): 2 days
- Pain B (DPP deadline, no technical leadership): 2 days
- Pain C (Supply chain data in spreadsheets): 2 days
- Pain D (Can't ship AI features fast enough): 1 day
- Rotate which pain point gets the single day each week

## Monthly Review Prompt

When generating a new month, consider:
- Which topics/pillars got the most engagement last month?
- Any new conversations that revealed a new pain point or angle?
- Rotate winning pillar to higher frequency next month
- Are there trending topics in FashionTech/DPP/sustainability space to ride?
- Has anything from the blog post queue already been covered? Skip it.
